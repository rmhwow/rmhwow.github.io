How Does the Internet Work?

Defining the Internet

We all know about the internet, but most people do not know how it actually works. The Internet is essentially a big wire in the ground. There are many different hardwards that support the Internet including routers, servers, and smartphones. These elements create networks. Client- this is the element that is receiving the information. Your personal computer or smartphone would be considered a client. Our computers are not directly linked to the internet but secondly handly linked through an Intern Service Provider. Servers- the Machines that Store webapges and information. These are the computers that are directly connected to the Internet. Routers- pack up the information and send the information to the correct area.

Servers have unique Internet Protocol (IP) addresses so this way they can communicate with one another. Because these addresses aren't easy to remember we also give them names such as "google.com", "yahoo.com", and "facebook.com". So we go on our personal computer and with the help of an ISP - maybe an example would be DSL we access aol.com.
